Storms in the Written Word

There are many different types of storms.  Storms have not only shaped the earth geologically; they have also shaped literature for generations.

"Storms don't come to teach us painful lessons, rather they were meant to wash us clean" - Shannon L. Adler

"Life isn't about waiting for the storm to pass...Life is about learning to dance in the rain" - Vivian Greene

"The fishermen know that the sea is dangerous and the storm terrible, but they have never found these dangers sufficient reason for remaining ashore" - Vincent Van Gogh

"One friend in a storm is worth more than a thousand friends in the sunshine" - Matshona Dhliwayo

 

Storms bring with them chaos and uncertainty. Often it's difficult to say how terrible the storm will be and what the aftermath will look like. Storms are often used to represent a difficult or intense period in a person's life. 

Because of this uncertainty and chaos storms have become symbolic.  Storms have been steeped in literature for as long as the written word.
